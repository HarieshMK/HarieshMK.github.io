Who needs an emergency fund? What is it? Why does every finance YouTuber shout about having 3 to 6 months of expenses saved away?

Most of them tell you what to do, but few explain what a real emergency looks like. Let’s strip away the noise and deep-dive into the one thing that fumbles many.

## Who Needs This?
Before we start, let’s answer the most important question: “Is this for me?”

If you are backed by a massive family inheritance, have a wealthy background, or have friends who can gift you lakhs of rupees at 2 AM without asking questions—you might not need this. (Though I’d still suggest you build one for the sake of independence).

For everyone else? **This is non-negotiable.**

## What is a Real Emergency?
A new phone launches with the specs of your dreams. Is that an emergency?

You find a massive off-season discount for a trip to the Maldives. Is that an emergency?

Maybe you want to pay off a credit card bill or prepay a loan. Emergency?

**WRONG.**

Emergency funds are for the things life throws at you when you aren't looking:

* A family member is rushed to the hospital.
* Your employer suddenly fires you (I pray it should never happen).
* Your bike engine seizes in the middle of the road.
* A major home appliance breaks down, making life impossible.

These are real emergencies. They don't give you a heads-up, and you won't have a specific "budget" for them. You cannot move forward without incurring these expenses. That’s what a real emergency is, and that’s when this fund gives you a hand.

## How Much Should You Save?
The standard rule is 3 months of salary or 6 months of expenses. If your monthly expense is ₹50,000, you’re looking at a ₹3 Lakh goal.

“But Hariesh, I can’t find ₹3 Lakhs today! I’ll just wait until I get a huge hike.”

**WRONG AGAIN.** You don’t need the full amount on Day 1. Start small. Even ₹1,000 a month is a victory. Read my previous blog(https://harieshmk.github.io/blogs/how-to-start-investment-journey/) I shared earlier to see how much you can divert toward this fund.

**Pro-Tip:** If you work in a high-risk industry (like a startup), aim for 6 to 9 months. Uncertainty requires a thicker cushion.

## The Insurance Lie:
“Hariesh, I have Corporate Health Insurance and Car/Bike Insurance. Why do I need a separate fund?”

Nope, wrong again. We live in a world where insurance rarely covers 100%. You will almost always face "deductibles," "non-medical charges," or "processing fees" that can range from 10% to 20% of the total bill. I’m speaking from personal experience here. To get that 90% covered, you need the cash to pay the 10% gap.

## Where Should You Keep It? (My Blueprint)
I don’t keep it in one asset class. Below is the breakdown of my current allocation:

* **Fixed Deposit/RD:** I locked this in at 7.05% when rates were high. If you can get >6%, it's worth it. If it's less, skip it. I have 30% of my emergency fund here. Once this RD matures, I will not go for new RDs (because I will not find attractive interest rates anymore). I will push this money into the arbitrage fund.
* **Liquid Fund:** Currently giving me around 6.3%. It’s safer than equity but better than a 3% savings account. Currently, this is also around 30% of my EF.
* **Arbitrage Fund:** My secret for tax optimization (want to know how? Subscribe below and soon I will post a blog about it). Currently, this gives me 6.7%. 40% of my EF is present here.

**Pro-Tip:** If you don’t want to split, I’d suggest keeping everything in a Liquid fund.

## The Trump Card:
You might ask: *"Hariesh, if my money is in a Mutual Fund, I can’t withdraw it at 2 AM for an emergency!"*

That’s where the **Credit Card** comes in. Most emergencies can be swiped. I use the card to pay immediately, then I initiate the withdrawal from my funds. The money hits my bank in 2-3 days, and I pay off the card bill on time.

**WARNING:** This requires serious discipline. Credit cards are only a "hack" if you aren't a serial defaulter. (Stay tuned—I'll be posting a guide on using Credit Cards responsibly soon).

## One Final Rule: Refill the Tank
Once the storm passes and you’ve used your fund, pause your other investments until you fill back the spent amount, so that the emergency fund is full again.

Your future self will thank you for starting today. **Trust me.**
