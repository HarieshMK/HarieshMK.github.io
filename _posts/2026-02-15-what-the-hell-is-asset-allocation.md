## What the hell is asset allocation?  
Does that really matter? What should I be aware of? Why is it not as complicated as it sounds? Let’s break it down.

Let’s start with a recent example. Recently, gold and silver rallied hard. Many people felt the FOMO and got the urge to jump in because prices were moving up fast. Some even increased their allocation heavily. When silver corrected sharply (more than 50%), portfolios bled red, confidence dropped, and a few people lost interest in investing altogether. If you want the detailed breakdown of what happened with silver, refer to this blog:  
https://harieshmk.github.io/2026/02/02/the-silver-rally-and-crash/

There is nothing wrong with wanting high returns. What’s wrong is going **all in** on one idea. That’s where asset allocation comes in.

### So, what the hell is asset allocation?
Asset allocation is simply how you split your money across different asset classes while investing.

### What is an asset class?
Think of asset classes as buckets where your money can go:

- **Equity** (stocks / equity mutual funds): Grow your money over the long term  
- **Debt** (FDs, RDs, bonds, liquid & debt funds): Stability and downside protection  
- **Metals** (gold, silver): Hedge against uncertainty (not guaranteed protection)  
- **Cash**: Liquidity and mental comfort during chaos  

Each asset class behaves differently in different market conditions. That’s the whole point.

### Why asset allocation matters
In a bull market, a person with 100% equity will usually make more money than someone with 60% equity. But when markets crash, the 100% equity person is also the first one to panic, sell, and walk away from investing. The second person, with debt or metals cushioning the fall, is more likely to stay invested.

Asset allocation is not about maximizing short-term returns. It’s about maximizing **your time in the market**. And time in the market beats perfect timing every single time.

Remember: **Behavior beats intelligence in investing.**

### Why people think asset allocation is complicated
Because they consume content not meant for them. YouTube creators talk about everything—international funds, REITs, commodities, thematic funds, smart beta, and 20 types of portfolios. Their job is to talk about everything. Your job is to filter what applies to you.

### How much allocation should you follow?
There is no perfect formula. But if you’re starting out, use this as a **starting framework**, not a rulebook:

- **Equity:** 60–70%  
- **Debt:** 25–35%  
- **Gold/Silver:** 0–5%  
- **Cash:** Small buffer  

Not more. You can always reduce or tweak based on your comfort and behavior. If you’re confused about where to invest within these buckets, this might help:  
https://harieshmk.github.io/2026/02/01/where-should-i-invest/

### Common beginner mistakes
- Going 100% equity because “I’m investing for the long term”  
- Keeping 0% in debt because “FDs don’t give returns”  
- Allocating 25–30% to gold because “gold is safe”  
- Constantly changing allocation multiple times a year (taxes + costs quietly kill returns)

### Thumb rules to remember
- Asset allocation > best-performing fund  
- Decide allocation first, then choose funds  
- Don’t change allocation because of headlines like “Gold will double in 2 years”  
- Rebalance only if needed, and at most once a year  

Coming back to silver: imagine someone had 30–40% of their portfolio in silver because it was going up. When silver corrected sharply, the portfolio damage would have been brutal and emotionally crushing. If silver was just 3–5% of the portfolio, the same crash would feel like an inconvenience, not a disaster. That’s the difference asset allocation makes.

### Final words
Asset allocation will not make you rich overnight. But it will stop you from doing stupid things that make you poor over time.
